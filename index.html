Motion and 3D

Using motion graphics and 3D to enrich a brand

Chosen example
As an example I've chosen the Apple WWDC 2013 Keynote Intro video, as it is a perfect example of a very simple animation with a strong message and feel. This example shines because Apple products are visually strong yet minimalist and the video conveys exactly the same feeling.
What are motion graphics and why are they important?
Motion graphics are an incredibly powerful tool to enrich any brand. Motion graphics are a way to communicate with the viewer, and add depth to the story. Together with music and effective copy, they can give us a strong message. Motion graphics is animation, but with text as a major component. Essentially, it’s animated graphic design.

What are 3D graphics and why are they important?
3D is also a powerful tool and while it is similar to motion in a way this method of animating or telling a story can be even more powerful when we consider the possibilities of more realistic or even more complex visual experiences. From life like mockups that are 3d modeled to the millimiter to infinitely complex particle simulations 3D offers an endless world of possibilities that can enrich a brand.

Design of reference

What do I consider design of reference?
I consider that a company has a design of reference when visually you automatically know what you are looking at and exactly how it impacts you. In my opinion google is a really good example of design that is not only user friendly but also has a strong brand image attached to it. The same goes for companies like Apple, Microsoft and many others. There are examples outside of the technology field, like Coca-cola and many others, but the same goes for all of them. Illustration systems

Illustration systems

Increasingly, illustration is being used to express brand value, especially on the web. To be clear, companies are going beyond commissioning one-off illustrations that highlight isolated talking points on their websites. A brand illustration system is a collection of images with a cohesive mood and style that clarifies a brand’s promise, often with a nod to human experience (humor, hope, irony, etc.). The first answer is language. An illustration system is a way to enrich a brand’s visual language and say more nuanced things that a logo, color scheme, typeface, or even words cannot convey alone. The second answer is consistency. Consistent branding (i.e., promise making) creates expectations for a business to meet, and people reward met expectations with loyalty. A brand illustration system gives a business the ability to unfold its brand story with consistency on multiple platforms (web, print, social, etc).
(Micah Bowers, Brand Illustration 101: Visualizing the Narrative)

Creative process

What is my UX process?
My process will depend on the task, for UX and UI I use the reference process. I start by understanding the problem by gathering requirements, creating personas, defining use cases, etc. Researching helps me by analyzing competitors, researching the latest UX trends and guidelines. Skteching helps me to gather ideas and have an early vision of what I intend. A design phase where I create mockups and define the UX guidelines. Implementation where the experience is implemented. Lastly the evaluation where I performa usability testing, reports and identify improvements.
What is my 3D process?
For 3D there's a much more technical aspect as there are knowledge and hardware requirements to completing a task. The creative process is identical in it's nature but it differs in approach. For example the sketching phase should be the storyboard phase. The evaluation phase should ideally be before the implementation and it should be done by doing rough cuts, animatics or any other means that can represent the final vision to the team. Lastly the pipeline of work will depend on the final goal. Are we adding 3D objects to a video? In that case multimedia content is required, camera tracking is required, compositing, maybe rotoscoping. The approach will essentially depend on the nature of the content, hardware and number of people working on it.
